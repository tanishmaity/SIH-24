Dhokra Art

Dhokra art is an ancient form of metal casting that is traditionally practiced by tribal artisans in various parts of India, including Jamshedpur, Jharkhand. Known for its intricate craftsmanship and deep cultural roots, Dhokra art is one of the earliest known methods of non-ferrous metal casting, primarily using the lost-wax technique. The art form has been passed down through generations, often by the tribal communities such as the Dokra Kamars, who are skilled in this unique craft.

In Jamshedpur and surrounding tribal regions, Dhokra art is more than just an artistic expression; it is a way of life. The craft reflects the close relationship that the tribal communities have with nature and their surroundings. Dhokra art pieces often tell stories of everyday life, religious beliefs, and the simplicity of tribal existence.
